A trusted public space for the internet
We believe the internet should serve people, not exploit them.
Dot-Public exists to create a civic layer of the internet where public institutions can be trusted to act openly and honestly, and where people’s privacy and dignity are respected. It aims to free public services from commercial influence, make trustworthy spaces easy to recognise, and build shared digital infrastructure for the common good.
Dot-Public envisions a civic space online—a place where citizens, public institutions, and civil society can share trusted information and essential services without fear, noise, or hidden agendas. We’re building a structural safeguard for democratic life, so that the next generation inherits an internet worthy of public trust.
Dot-Public’s Missions
  1. Institutional Trust
    Civic institutions behave honestly, transparently, and reliably.
  2. User Privacy and Respect
    Individuals are protected from hidden tracking and exploitation.
  3. Independence from Commercial Infrastructure
    Public institutions aren’t forced into commercial platforms’ logic.
  4. Clarity and Legibility of Public Space
    People can recognise and trust public-service spaces online.
  5. Shared Civic Infrastructure
    Public-interest organisations have common tools and rules to work together.
