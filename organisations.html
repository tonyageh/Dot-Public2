Dot-Public Membership Criteria

Dot-Public membership is open to organisations that:

  • Operate with a primary mission of public service or cultural preservation
  • Are not majority controlled by private, profit-seeking entities
  • Are accountable to the public through legal, financial, or ethical standards
  • Publish information, services, or works intended for public benefit

Our Mission

Dot-Public is dedicated to helping people to rediscover trust and confidence in the information produced and published by organisations with an explicit mission to serve the public, in particular those that fall into one or more of the following sectors:

  • Government and State Institutions
  • Cultural and Heritage Institutions
  • Academic and Research Institutions
  • Arts and Creative Organisations
  • Libraries, Archives, and Digital Repositories
  • Science and Technology Organisations
  • Civic and Social Advocacy Organisations
  • Regulatory and Standards Bodies
  • Community and Local Heritage Initiatives
