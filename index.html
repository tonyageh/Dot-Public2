Dot-Public

A civic layer for the internet

Dot-Public is an initiative to create a public-interest digital infrastructure. It is not a platform. It is not a product. It is a permissioned top-level domain – .public – designed to support trust, accountability and civic engagement online.

Participation is voluntary and permissioned. Institutions that use the Dot-Public domain must adhere to clear governance rules, transparency standards and public service principles. They are not tracking users or monetising behaviour. They are here to serve.

The goal is simple: to create a trustworthy space online where public institutions and communities can operate with dignity, safety and mutual respect.
